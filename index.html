“What one fool can understand, another can.”

- Richard P. Feynman

The Feynman technique is a popular study method in which you learn through the act of teaching. Richard Feynman, a renowned physicist believed that you understand a concept better when you teach it.

This technique is a very effective method for studying but one of the biggest shortcomings is finding a listener who is interested in learning the topic you are going to explain.

Use our website to follow the steps of the Feynman technique and learn from one another.

Begin by grabbing a sheet of paper and writing the concept simply in your own words.

Create a post in our community feed showcasing the topic you would like to make sure you understand.

Find willing listeners and teach them the concept.

Find out how well you understand your topic by talking to someone who might not know much about your topic.

Review your explanation and identify areas you need help understanding.

Rating for how well you explained your topic will show after meeting.

Go back to your notes and enforce your understanding in those areas.

Keep explaining the concept as much as you can until you feel comfortable with your understanding.
